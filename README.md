## About Me

I am Wahb Mohamed, a computer science student with a strong focus on **applied artificial intelligence**, particularly **deep learning, generative models, computer vision, and natural language processing**.

My work emphasizes **deep understanding over surface-level usage**. I aim to understand models both mathematically and practically, analyze their behavior during training, and deploy them as part of reliable, explainable systems. I am especially interested in complex architectures such as **GANs and Transformers**, where stability, interpretability, and design choices matter.

---

## Current Work & Interests

- **Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)**  
  Hands-on work with DCGAN, WGAN, Conditional GANs, and CycleGAN.  
  Focus areas include training stability, loss behavior, normalization strategies, and real-world applications such as image translation and media analysis.

- **Computer Vision**  
  CNN-based models for image and video understanding, with practical experience in face-related tasks and **deepfake detection**, including multimodal approaches combining visual and audio signals.

- **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**  
  Text preprocessing pipelines, Word2Vec embeddings, sentiment classification, and evaluation using confusion matrices.  
  Expanding into **Transformer-based models** and attention mechanisms with an emphasis on architectural understanding.

- **Transformers & Modern Architectures**  
  Studying Transformer models from first principles, including attention, scaling behavior, and training dynamics, and exploring their role in multimodal AI systems.

- **End-to-End AI Systems**  
  Building complete AI pipelines—from data preprocessing and model training to APIs, evaluation, and explainability—rather than isolated experiments.

---

## Learning Philosophy

I prioritize **strong foundations**, followed by implementation and iteration.  
A model is not considered understood unless its behavior, limitations, and trade-offs can be clearly explained.
